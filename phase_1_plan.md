# Phase 1 Implementation Plan: Establish the Baseline

## Introduction
This guide provides actionable, step-by-step instructions for the business owner of Blade & Comb to execute Phase 1 of the marketing strategy: Establishing the Baseline for SEO and the Website. You do not need any prior marketing experience to follow these steps.

## Step 1: Claim and Optimize Google Business Profile
Your Google Business Profile is what appears when people search for "barbershop near me" or "Blade and Comb Aurora" on Google or Google Maps.

1. **How to Claim Your Profile:**
   - Go to Google and search for "Blade and Comb Aurora CO".
   - If a business listing appears on the right side of the search results, look for a link that says "Own this business?". Click it and follow the steps to verify ownership (usually through a phone call, text, or email).
   - If the listing doesn't exist, go to `google.com/business`, click "Manage now," and follow the prompts to add your business.
2. **How to Optimize Your Basic Information:**
   - Once logged in, click "Edit profile."
   - Ensure your address (Aurora, CO), phone number, and business hours match exactly what is on your website. Consistency across the internet is very important for Google to trust your business.
3. **How to Add High-Quality Photos:**
   - On the Google Business dashboard, click "Add photo."
   - You don't need a professional photographer; modern smartphones (like newer iPhones or Samsung Galaxys) are fine. Ensure the lenses are clean and the lighting in the shop is bright.
   - Upload 5-10 clear, welcoming photos of the shop's interior.
   - Upload 5-10 exterior photos showing the storefront and parking so customers can easily find you.
   - Upload 10+ high-quality photos of your best haircuts (fades, beard trims, etc.). Make sure to ask the client's permission before posting their photo.
4. **Link to Website and Booking:**
   - Under "Edit profile," find the "Website" field and add `https://www.bladeandcomb.com/`.
   - Find the "Booking" or "Appointments" section and paste the direct link to your Squire booking portal so customers can book immediately from Google.

## Step 2: Implement a Google Review Strategy
Your competitors have 600-1,000+ reviews. Building up your review count is the most critical step for getting new customers in the door.

1. **Set a Goal:** Aim for 100 new 5-star reviews in the next 3 months to build momentum.
2. **How to Get Your Review Link:**
   - Go to your Google Business Profile dashboard. Look for a button that says "Ask for reviews" or "Get more reviews." Click it to copy your unique short link (it will look something like `g.page/r/.../review`).
3. **Automate the Ask (Squire Integration):**
   - Log into your Squire dashboard or point-of-sale system.
   - Look for "Marketing," "Automations," or "Client Engagement." 
   - Set up an automated SMS (text message) or email to send 2 hours after an appointment: *"Thanks for visiting Blade & Comb! If you loved your cut, please leave us a quick review here: [Paste your Google Review Link]"*.
4. **The Verbal Ask (Training Staff):**
   - Train all barbers to end a great appointment by asking for a review while the client is still in the chair. Use a phrase like: *"I'm glad you like the cut! It would mean a lot to me and the shop if you could drop a quick Google review. I'll text you the link right now."* (Then text them the link from step 2).
5. **How to Get QR Codes:**
   - Go to a free website like `qr-code-generator.com`.
   - Paste your Google Review link from Step 2 into the URL field. Download the resulting QR code image.
   - Take the image to a local print shop (like FedEx Office or VistaPrint online) and have them print small, branded cards or mirror decals. Place these at the register or at each barber station.

## Step 3: Upgrade the Website Content for Local SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
SEO means making small changes to your website so Google understands what you do and where you are, helping you appear higher in search results. If you don't know how to edit your website, you may need to ask the person who built it or learn the basics of your website builder (like Squarespace, Wix, or WordPress).

1. **Update the Homepage Text:**
   - Add a prominent, welcoming headline.
   - Example Addition: *"Blade & Comb: The Premier Barbershop serving Southeast Aurora, Parker, and the Denver Metro Area."* Adding these city names helps Google connect you to local searches.
   - Make sure words like "men's haircuts," "fades," and "hot towel shaves" are written out naturally on the homepage.
2. **Create a 'Meet the Team' Section:**
   - Add a section on the website with a short (2-3 sentence) biography for each master barber and stylist.
   - Include clear headshots.
   - Highlight their specialties to give customers confidence in booking.
3. **Add a Visual Gallery:**
   - Create a new "Gallery" page on the site.
   - Upload the exact same high-quality haircut photos used for the Google Business Profile. 
   - *Important SEO Trick:* Before uploading the photos, rename the image files on your computer to describe what they are (e.g., name the file `aurora-fade-haircut.jpg` instead of `IMG_1234.jpg`). Google cannot "see" images, but it reads the file names.
4. **Prepare for Instagram Integration (Prep for Phase 2):**
   - Plan to add an Instagram feed to your website footer. Most website builders have a simple "Instagram Block" you can drag and drop onto the page. Once the business Instagram profile is created in Phase 2, connect it so fresh photos appear on the website automatically.

## Next Steps
Once these steps are fully implemented, you will have a rock-solid, professional digital baseline that converts people searching online into booking customers. When Step 3 is complete, you are ready to move on to Phase 2: Launch Visual Social Media (Instagram & TikTok).
